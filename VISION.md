# ReportsTo — Vision

## Why this exists

I've had managers who changed my career. People who saw where I was going before I did, who put my name in rooms I wasn't in, who went to bat for me when it cost them something. I've also had the other kind.

That's not a performance story. That's a coin flip.

I'm a product manager. I know how to research a company before I join it. I can gather financial data, read company reviews, talk to people in my network. And yet every time I've evaluated a new role, the most important question — who am I actually going to work for? — was the one I could answer least. A 30-minute interview. A cherry-picked reference from their favorite former report. A LinkedIn profile that tells me where they've been, not what it was like to work for them.

Your career shouldn't be left up to luck. You deserve to know about the person who has the potential to make or break it.

*— Megan Moore, Founder*

---

**Your manager will do more for your career — or to it — than almost anyone else. What if you could understand what kind of leader they are before you decide to work for them?**

## What it is

ReportsTo is a verified review platform for the people you work for — not companies, not teams, not cultures. Individual leaders. Managers and executives at every level, rated across observable behavioral dimensions by the people who actually worked with them — whether they reported directly or worked alongside them as a cross-functional partner.

The core question the product answers: **who is this person I might work for, and how do they lead?**

Not whether the company has good snacks. Not whether the culture is "collaborative." Whether this specific human being protects their team, communicates clearly, gives credit, follows through on what they say, grows the people under them, and stays consistent when things get hard — and whether that picture holds up from every professional angle, not just from below.

---

## Who it's for

ReportsTo is built for people who are intentional about the leadership environment they're walking into. They're not just searching for a job — they're searching for the right person to work for. They know from experience that a great role under a bad leader is a bad role.

Not "job seekers" broadly. People who understand that **who you report to is the single most important variable in your career experience** — and who are motivated enough to act on that understanding.

This includes people who never reported to this manager directly. A cross-functional partner who was repeatedly burned by a peer manager's broken commitments has signal that no direct report can provide. A skip-level employee who watched a VP absorb their team's credit in leadership meetings sees something the direct reports cannot. ReportsTo is built for anyone who has worked *with* a manager in any professional capacity — because the complete picture of a leader requires more than one vantage point.

## Why existing platforms fail

**Glassdoor** reviews companies. A bad VP gets buried in aggregate company sentiment. The question "what is it like to work for this specific person" is unanswerable.

**Blind** is unstructured and unverified. Former employees stay verified indefinitely. Free-text anonymous platforms become noise, and noise cannot be trusted.

**Rate My Manager** covers only engineering managers and has no verification layer or behavioral taxonomy.

None of them answer the actual question.

The accountability gap is real. Executives and managers are the only professional class with almost no external accountability layer. Boards protect them. HR reports to them. 360 reviews are internal and buried. The whisper network — asking carefully, because the power dynamic makes it a career risk — is the only mechanism most people have.

ReportsTo makes the whisper network into a structured, verifiable, public record.

Existing platforms also only capture one vantage point. A manager who is beloved by their direct reports but adversarial to every adjacent team is a specific and common failure mode that no platform surfaces. A 360 review might catch it internally — but it's buried, non-public, and controlled by the same organization that protects the manager. ReportsTo is the first platform where both direct report and cross-functional perspectives exist in one structured profile, labeled and displayed separately so a job seeker can see the complete picture.

## What makes it trustworthy

**Verification is structural.** Every reviewer must prove they worked for the person they're rating. Current employees verify via work email confirmation. Former employees verify via paycheck upload — first and most recent paycheck from that employer, proving tenure without requiring an exact end date. Manager employment windows are cached from the first reviewer's verification and reused for subsequent reviewers with overlapping tenure, reducing friction without sacrificing rigor. You cannot review someone you never worked with.

**Anonymity is architectural, not promised.** Reviewer identity and review content never exist in the same database row. Verification happens in one system. The review lives in another. A one-way token bridges them only long enough to confirm eligibility. After that, the bridge burns. Even we cannot reverse it.

**Structure is the filter.** Six specific, observable behavioral dimensions replace a free-text box. It is very hard to defame someone on "follows through on commitments" without the review revealing more about the reviewer than the person being rated. The format is the moderation.

## The reviewer model

ReportsTo recognizes two distinct reviewer relationships, each with genuine but different observational access.

**Direct reports** witnessed the full power-dynamic relationship: how this person managed down, distributed credit, protected their team, developed and advocated for their people.

**Cross-functional partners** witnessed the externally-facing behavior: whether this person followed through on commitments that affected other teams, communicated decisions across org boundaries, and showed up consistently in settings they didn't control.

A manager can be an exceptional advocate for their direct reports while being adversarial to every adjacent team. That gap is real, common, and currently invisible. ReportsTo surfaces it by routing reviewers to the question set that matches their vantage point — and displaying both perspectives separately on the profile.

---

## What we are not building

ReportsTo is not a complaint platform. It is an information platform. The distinction matters in every decision — how reviews are structured, how the brand communicates, how we moderate. A 2 out of 5 on protecting the team is not a violation. It's the product working.

We will never allow leaders or companies to manage, suppress, purchase visibility over, or respond to reviews. Trust is earned before it is monetized.
